# Theory of Learning

A unified model of skill acquisition, from first perception through full embodiment. Adapted from other frameworks but developed here in Dan's own vocabulary. The model describes five overlapping phases, not a clean stage progression — they run in parallel and feed each other.

---

## Stage Sequence

### 1. Discernment

The first phase. You are tickling the existing perceptual apparatus to orient toward a new gradient — one you were either never aware of, or never learned to navigate.

This is not yet control. It is not even directed search. It is just beginning to sense that a gradient exists: slightly more, slightly less, slightly better, slightly worse, slightly higher, slightly lower.

In violin: you put down your third finger, it's slightly off, and you wiggle it up and down — exploring the range of motion on the axes that constrain this action set. You're trying to develop your ear to hear "too far, a little higher, ah, I can't tell." It's hard at first. That's discernment.

The Inner Game of Tennis is a touchstone here. The same perceptual-before-technical logic applies.

Discernment is primarily embodied. Trying to put words to what discernment is learning often does a disservice to the spatial, real-time, proprioceptive nature of the activity. This limitation of language is structural, not just communicative.

**What it requires:** Nothing prior. You need a body, a medium, and a gradient.

**What it enables:** Stumbling into coherence.

---

### 2. Coherence (Momentary)

Through discernment, you stumble into a brief state of coherence. This is not yet sustained — it is an instantaneous moment of multi-scale phase alignment.

You are pedaling and suddenly, just for a moment, you are fully on two wheels. The finger lands in exactly the right position and the whole instrument has sympathetic resonance — the frequency the finger is representing rings through the body of the violin. The swing is clean and the ball goes over the net, even if just once.

There is a felt quality to this moment. It registers as: *ah, that's it, yes* — a sense of rightness that carries almost a moral weight. This is not sentimental; it is a signal. The system has found something real.

Key structural point: you can access coherence with bad ergonomics. You can land a handstand once with terrible alignment. The coherence is still structural — it is still a real configuration of the system — even if it is not yet sustainable. That first taste is a useful anchor point for awareness, even if built on scaffolding that will need to change.

Coherence is always built on existing substrates. The violin example: the bow hold also has to be working, the body has to be breathing. You may be discerning multiple dimensions simultaneously without realizing it, stumbling into a multi-modal configuration that, at that particular moment in time with that particular posture, produces coherence.

**What it requires:** Enough discernment to accidentally land on the configuration.

**What it enables:** A taste of the target state. An anchor. The beginning of attunement.

---

### 3. Attunement

Attunement is the ability to sense where you are now and move toward where you want to go. It develops once you have the taste of coherence to aim at.

Where discernment is nominal — detecting that a gradient exists — attunement is directional and multi-dimensional. It is like tuning a radio across multiple knobs simultaneously, trying to find the frequency where things come into alignment.

The transition from discernment to attunement is not clean. They overlap. As you develop discernment on one axis, you are already beginning to attune on others. The taste of coherence — even accessed with bad ergonomics — provides an anchor. As ergonomic discernment develops, attunement becomes more fine-grained, and you learn multiple pathways to access the same state of coherence in ways that are fractally sound: stable, phase-aligned, ergonomic, energy-efficient, metabolically optimal.

The game then shifts. The question is no longer just "can I access coherence?" but "can I sustain it?" Sustaining coherence is a different phenomenon entirely from accessing it. It requires a new level of attunement — more layers, greater precision, traversing the possibility space of embodied proprioception across all the axes simultaneously.

To sustain a handstand for 30 seconds requires actively unlearning patterns and habits that don't serve, and repping — physically or imaginally — new patterns. It is a continuous, active, dynamic balancing act.

**What it requires:** A taste of coherence (anchor), and enough discernment to have developed directional sensitivity.

**What it enables:** Reliable access to coherence. Sustained coherence. The construction of encoded insights.

---

### 4. Encoded Insights

As attunement develops and coherence becomes accessible and then sustainable, something accumulates. Mental models get built. The body and mind — neurology, imagination, intuition, proprioception, thinking mind — get inscribed with knowledge accumulated through repetitive practice.

Encoded insights are not shortcuts. They are earned wisdom encoded for your unique system. They are the skills or prompts that allow you to calibrate and engage with these layers of coherence with ease — not because the system is being bypassed, but because the system has been trained.

These are the artifacts of learning. They are in phase alignment with the goal of accessing and sustaining coherence — which is to say, they are themselves expressions of coherence.

**What they require:** Cycles of attunement and coherence, across time, with sufficient rep density.

**What they enable:** Reduced attentional load. The scaffolding for embodiment.

---

### 5. Embodiment (Attention Cost → 0)

The final stage. The efficiency of searching, attuning, and sustaining has become so optimized that it approaches instantaneous. The attention required to access or sustain coherence approaches zero.

The master snowboarder gets on the board and goes. They don't need to think about muscle groups, lean angles, edge pressure, terrain response. The beginner is wiggling, uncertain, overwhelmed. That delta is embodiment.

When something is truly embodied, it becomes subconscious, autonomic, integrated — a new building block, a new vocabulary item, a new foundation that other skills can be built on top of. It is no longer a thing being done; it is a thing being.

Each stage — discernment, attunement, accessed coherence, sustained coherence, encoded insights, embodiment — is happening in parallel, not in sequence. But the arc is real: at the beginning, enormous attentional resources are required to hold even one dimension. At the end, the entire configuration runs for free.

**What it requires:** All prior stages, plus sufficient time and reps for the system to fully integrate.

**What it enables:** New base layer. Something to build on.

---

## Structural Claims

These are non-obvious assertions in the model — not just phenomenological description.

1. **Coherence accessible with bad ergonomics is real coherence.** It is a structural finding, not just an approximation. The bad ergonomics matter for sustainability, not for whether the first contact was genuine.

2. **Sustaining coherence is a different phenomenon from accessing it.** This is not a matter of degree. Getting to a state and holding a state engage different processes, require different attunement, and have different failure modes.

3. **Success triggers collapse.** When coherence is accessed and the system is not yet holding it automatically, the recognition of the desired outcome redirects attention toward the outcome itself, dropping the holding that was maintaining it. The system collapses at the moment of success. This is structural and predictable, not a beginner's failure.

4. **The human system can only focus on one thing at a time.** Multi-dimensional attunement is accomplished through attention juggling, not parallel processing. The load of juggling is real, and system collapse under attentional overload is a direct consequence.

5. **A mentor's primary lever is attentional redirection.** Not technique transmission — that's secondary. The core value of a good teacher is training the student's meta-attentional system: what to notice, when, and how to shift focus as circumstances change.

6. **Embodiment is a threshold, not a gradient.** Once something is truly embodied, the attentional cost goes to zero — it becomes a subconscious building block. This is qualitatively different from "getting better at it."

## Load-Bearing Vocabulary

**Discernment** — The initial phase of perceiving that a gradient exists, and beginning to sense its poles. Not control, not navigation — just orientation. Pre-verbal and proprioceptive at root.

**Attunement** — Directional sensitivity toward coherence, across multiple dimensions simultaneously. The radio-knob image is load-bearing: many axes, tuning in parallel, toward a target that is known by feel rather than specification.

**Coherence** — A state of multi-scale phase alignment. The experience is felt as "rightness" or "everything coming together." In physical domains: sympathetic resonance, clean swing, stable balance. Not metaphorical — literally a configuration where system-level alignment produces the felt quality.

**Sympathetic resonance** — Used here as metaphor for multi-system alignment. When the finger is in exactly the right place, the whole instrument rings with the frequency being represented. Extended: when all dimensions of a complex system are in phase, the whole system resonates.

**System collapse** — The failure mode that occurs when attentional load exceeds system capacity, OR when the system celebrates coherence and drops the holding that was sustaining it. Can be triggered by overwhelm (too many axes at once) or by success (attention redirected to the outcome).

**Attention juggling** — The mechanism by which humans simulate multi-dimensional focus. Not parallel attention — sequential rapid switching, with associated overhead and failure probability.

**Meta-attentional system** — The capacity to direct one's own attention: to know what to focus on, when to shift, how to hold multiple things without collapsing. A learnable skill, not a fixed trait. Good teachers primarily train this.

**Goldilocks window** — The zone of "just right" in any dimension — not too much, not too little. Multiple windows exist simultaneously, and coherence requires being in all of them at once. Fine, continual readjustment is required to remain in the intersection.

**Encoded insights** — Earned wisdom encoded into the system for the specific learner. Not shortcuts or bypasses — the real thing compressed into usable form. Emerge from cycles of attunement and coherence, across time.

**Embodiment (attention cost → 0)** — The terminal stage of skill acquisition where the attentional resources required to access or sustain coherence approach zero. The skill becomes subconscious, autonomic — a building block, not an act.

## Key Tensions and Paradoxes

**The attention paradox.** The moment you notice you have achieved coherence — when you think "I'm doing it!" — you drop the holding required to sustain it. Attention redirects from the process to the product, and the product disappears. This is not a beginner mistake; it is a structural property of systems that haven't yet embodied the thing. The resolution is not "don't notice" but "embody it deeply enough that noticing costs nothing."

**The ergonomics inversion.** You can access coherence with bad ergonomics. This means you may develop an anchor for the state before you have the ergonomic stability to sustain it. The anchor is still real and valuable — it gives you something to aim at — but the path from "I can touch it" to "I can hold it" runs through ergonomic development, not just more reps of the original access.

**The Goldilocks window.** Every axis has a zone of "just right." Sustaining coherence requires being in the Goldilocks window across all axes simultaneously, through continual dynamic readjustment. Too much focus on any one axis collapses others. Too little attention overall means drift. The window is real but narrow, and maintaining it is its own skill.

**Stage overlap and parallel development.** The model reads like a sequence, but every stage is running simultaneously. You are always discerning, attuning, accessing, sustaining, encoding, and embodying — just at different depths across different axes. This means progress is not linear and is hard to observe directly. A student may be far ahead on one axis while barely started on another.
